# Business Research Modes

When the research goal is business-oriented (not pure technology), select the matching mode below and use its checklist to guide sub-question planning. Combine these checklists with the search workflow in the main skill — they define *what to collect*, the main skill defines *how to search*.

## Mode 1: Investor / Fund Diligence

Collect:
- Fund size, stage focus, and typical check size
- Relevant portfolio companies (especially in the same domain)
- Public thesis and recent investment activity
- Reasons the fund is or is not a fit for the user's context
- Red flags or mismatches (stage mismatch, sector drift, controversy)

Output: decision-oriented dossier, not a Wikipedia summary. End with "fit / no fit / conditional fit" recommendation.

## Mode 2: Competitive Analysis

Collect:
- Product reality (what it actually does), not marketing copy
- Funding and investor history if public
- Traction metrics if available (revenue, users, growth rate)
- Distribution channels and pricing clues
- Strengths, weaknesses, and positioning gaps

Output: structured comparison table (feature matrix) + narrative analysis of each competitor's strategic position. End with "where the opportunity gaps are" for the user.

## Mode 3: Market Sizing (TAM/SAM/SOM)

Use two approaches and cross-check:
- **Top-down**: industry reports, analyst estimates, public datasets → total addressable market
- **Bottom-up**: realistic customer count × average revenue per customer → sanity check

Every number needs either a source or an explicit label as "estimate." Call out assumptions clearly — e.g., "assumes 5% penetration rate based on [source]."

Output: size estimate with confidence range, not a single number. Include growth trajectory if data exists.

## Mode 4: Technology / Vendor Evaluation

Collect:
- How it works (architecture, core mechanism)
- Trade-offs vs alternatives (performance, cost, complexity)
- Adoption signals (GitHub stars, community activity, enterprise customers)
- Integration complexity and migration path
- Lock-in risk, security posture, compliance certifications
- Operational burden (hosting, maintenance, monitoring)

Output: recommendation with trade-off matrix. End with "use when [X], avoid when [Y]."

## Quality Gate (Business Research)

Before delivering any business research report:
- [ ] All numbers are sourced or labeled as estimates
- [ ] Stale data (>12 months) is flagged
- [ ] Contrarian evidence and downside cases are included
- [ ] The recommendation follows from the evidence (not the other way around)
- [ ] Risks and counterarguments have their own section
